Research Areas

Network Robustness

City infrastructure systems, and road network resilience in particular, are a vital component of overall urban resilience. Transportation networks provide reliable mobility and effective delivery of critical services. However, our cities' ability to maintain essential functions is threatened by natural hazards disruptions (e.g., earthquake, hurricane, landslide, flooding etc.) and man-made disruption (e.g., terrorist attack, road closure etc.). From a practical point of view, robustness is a necessary condition to make a system resilient. I aim to develop simulation framework to systematically evaluate the network robustness under different disruption scenarios.

Connected/Autonomous Vehicle

Connected Vehicles (CV) are the leading edge of disruptive forces that will upend the traditional traffic composition, usher in new operation models, change the nature of traffic flow fundamentals and management, and further reshape the future of safety and mobility management. It is believed that CV technology has great potential to ease traffic congestion through the creation of a safe and interoperable connected vehicle network. I am interested in investigating the network performance during this transition regime with focus on traffic flow, network mobility, traffic safety, intelligent transportation system applications etc.

Network Mobility

Transportation infrastructure systems serve as the backbone of society to support the mobility of people and goods, connect businesses, and provide the support for supply chains and offer accessibility to vital resources for both daily activities and in emergency situations. In emergency situations, transportation networks play a vital role in evacuation, rescue operations, and community reconstruction, and recovery. Unplanned network disruption can result in significant disruptions on the transportation network, which eventually lead to connectivity and mobility loss. To ensure the network connectivity and accessibility to critical facilities, I will develop a large-scale simulation framework to investigate the mobility and accessibility under the influence of different types of network disruptions.

Network of Networks

As human societies continue to develop and expand, modern infrastructure systems become increasingly complex and interdependent. The modern society relies on a bewildering collection of complex and interwoven critical infrastructure systems. As technology advances, infrastructure networks are no longer isolated but rather a connected system consisting of various components interacting with each other in ways which is often not visible. As a result, the functionality of one infrastructure network not only depends on its own components, but also affected by the other networks coupled with it. Failure in one network is likely to result in failures to its dependent/interdependent networks, and vice-versa. This recursive process will eventually lead to cascading failures throughout the network of networks system. To prevent the network breaking apart, I aim to develop a network-of-network simulation framework to characterize the propagation of cascading failures among coupled networks and assess the network robustness, and identify the critical percolation transition threshold on the network robustness, which can be used to inform decision-making to strategically allocate and prioritize retrofitting resources to enhance network resilience.
